中文版本 / Traditional Chinese
我們看見你的堅強
親愛的宏福苑受災鄰舍，
2025年11月26日下午的那一場大火，改變了我們所有人。香港77年來最嚴重的火災，奪走了159條寶貴的生命。當七棟樓宇被吞噬在烈火中時，我們的城市陷入了無比的悲傷。
但在這段最黑暗的日子裡，我們也看到了什麼叫做人性的光輝。
William李先生的故事：絕望中的堅持
40歲的William Li當天在家。他是王昌樓——第一棟起火的大樓——二樓的居民。
當妻子打來電話告訴他有火災時，他立即準備逃生。但當他打開門的一刻，什麼都改變了。
他寫下自己的親身經歷：「黑暗籠罩著我，濃煙吞沒了我。我試圖打開手機手電筒，但能見度為零，呼吸變得令人難以承受。」
他關上門，退回房內。緊急逃生通道被切斷了——大堂已經變成火海。「在那一刻，我知道我最後的逃脫路線被切斷了。我會被困在這個叫『家』的煉獄裡，只能無奈而被動地等待。等待救援。」
此時，一般人會放棄。但William沒有。
他冷靜下來，找到毛巾，盡可能地將它們浸濕。然後，他聽到走廊外傳來鄰居的哭喊聲。他沒有猶豫，拿著濕毛巾衝出門，冒著被烈火吞沒的危險，將兩位鄰居拉進自己的房間。
他在接受CNN採訪時說，他被困了超過2.5小時，與兩位鄰居一起對抗窒息的濃煙和落下的碎片。他說：「我們的精神更堅強。」（Our spirit is tougher.）
直到消防員終於救出他們時，William才敢相信自己能活著看見明天。
但也正是因為William的決定——那個衝出門、不計生死去拯救陌生人的決定——兩條命今天還活著。
這就是香港人。在最危急的時刻，我們選擇了他人而非自己。
致敬：消防員何偉豪
在我們讚頌倖存者的同時，我們也必須記住那些給了一切的人。
37歲的消防員何偉豪於下午3時01分到達現場，是首批救援隊伍的一員。他隸屬沙田消防局，擔任「細搶」隊員，負責操作小型救援車。在王Fuk法院，他被分配到地下進行救援和滅火工作。
他加入消防處約9年。同袍稱他為「大隻豪」，形容他為人熱心、工作認真。加入消防處前，他曾是一名警務人員。
大約3時30分，何偉豪失去了與同袍的聯絡。
消防員們搜索了約30分鐘後，在宏昌樓升降機對開空地找到他時，他已經昏迷倒地，面部嚴重燒傷。救護員立即為他施予急救，並將他緊急運送到威爾斯親王醫院。
但在下午4時45分，何偉豪不幸不治身亡。
何偉豪原定在12月與相戀10年的未婚妻結婚。他的未婚妻在社交媒體上寫道：「我的超級英雄已完成他的任務。」
消防處處長楊恩健說：「何偉豪一直克盡厥職，表現英勇。我為痛失這位盡忠職守的同事感到極度難過和遺憾。我代表部門全體同事，向何偉豪的家人致以最深切的慰問。」
香港經歷了她最大的悲傷，但也見證了最純粹的英勇。
何偉豪，我們永遠不會忘記你。
超過2,000名救援人員的夜晚
在這場香港歷來最大規模的緊急救援行動中，超過2,000名消防員和救援人員衝入火海。
他們沒有退縮。他們沒有考慮自己的安全。他們只想拯救生命。
正是這2,000多個人，在43小時的不停救火工作中，在火勢蔓延至七棟樓宇時，救出了數百人。甚至在火災發生29小時後，他們仍然在16樓的樓梯間找到並救出了一名生還者——那是他們堅持不懈的見證。
社區的力量：從第一夜開始就在這裡
而這還不是全部。當消防員們在對抗烈火時，香港的普通市民也在行動。
在王福苑旁的露天廣場，短短三天內就出現了一個繁忙的救援中心。數百名志願者——大多數是透過Telegram和WhatsApp召集的陌生人——在組織捐贈物資。
來自各行各業、各個年齡層的志願者齊心協力：有人負責分類物資、有人負責追蹤物品清單、有人負責分發物品。一位名叫Jay的核心志願者，在她20多歲的年紀，統籌了整個志願者網絡。她說：「一些志願者擔當了每一類捐贈物資的『負責人』角色。他們用Google表單追蹤我們的物資，並報告仍然需要什麼。」
在這個黑暗的時刻，我們看到了什麼叫做「香港人的團結」。

•	免費心理輔導課程已為受影響學生展開
康復之路：你不是一個人
心理學家告訴我們，災難後的復元不是一條直線。有好日子，也會有難熬的日子。這都是正常的。你不是第一個經歷這種感受的人。
對於仍在悲傷中的人：
你的眼淚是必要的。允許自己去感受失去、去感受憤怒、去感受恐懼。但請記住，專業的心理學家、熱心的志願者、和整個社區都站在你身邊。
香港各大慈善機構已設立24小時支援：
•	撒瑪利亞防止撇棄生命會：24小時危機干預熱線
•	明愛：為受災者和家屬提供免費輔導服務
•	香港紅十字會：心理急救和長期創傷支援
對於正在重建的人：
很多居民現已入住臨時房屋或與親友同住。這是重新開始的機會。
Save the Children Hong Kong正在與香港樹仁大學心理學系合作，為受影響的學童提供長期心理支援。同時，多個NGO組織也在協助家庭取回失去的文件、重新領取身份證等必需的官方文件。
**政府已成立獨立委員會，由法官主持，審視起火及迅速蔓延的原因。警方已拘捕15人，包括建築公司高管和涉嫌停用火警警報的人士。**真相和正義正在被追求。

寫在最後
宏福苑的朋友們，
你們經歷了人生中最黑暗的時刻。你們失去了家、失去了摯愛、失去了安全感。這些損失是真實的，疼痛也是真實的。
但請看看圍繞著你們的一切：
•	William Li的故事，告訴我們人類有多麼偉大

在灰燼中，我們看到了香港最好的一面。在絕望中，我們找到了希望。你們的復元，將激勵著我們所有人。
讓我們一起，從今天開始重新站起來。
William說得好：「我們的精神更堅強。」
正是這樣的精神，推動著我們向前。

English Version
We See Your Strength
Dear families affected by the Wang Fuk Court fire,
The massive fire that swept through our Tai Po community on the afternoon of November 26, 2025, changed all of us. Hong Kong's deadliest blaze in 77 years claimed 159 precious lives. As seven residential blocks were engulfed in flames, our city was consumed by unprecedented sorrow.
But amid this darkest hour, we witnessed something extraordinary—the brilliance of human spirit and compassion.
The Story of William Li: Perseverance in Desperation
William Li, 40, was at home that day. He lived on the second floor of Wang Cheong House—the block that caught fire first.
When his wife called to warn him of the fire, he immediately prepared to escape. But the moment he opened his door, everything changed.
He wrote of his personal account: "Darkness engulfed me, as thick smoke swallowed me whole. I tried to activate my mobile's torch, but visibility was zero and breathing became excruciatingly difficult."
He closed the door and retreated. His emergency escape route was cut off—the lobby had become a sea of flames. "In that instant, I knew my final escape route was cut off. I would be imprisoned within this purgatory called 'home.' I could only wait helplessly and passively. Wait for rescue."
Most people would have given up. But William didn't.
He calmed himself, found towels, and wetted them as much as possible. Then he heard cries from neighbors in the corridor outside. Without hesitation, he grabbed the wet towels and rushed out, risking his own life to pull two neighbors into his apartment.
When interviewed by CNN, he described being trapped for more than 2.5 hours with his two neighbors, battling suffocating smoke and falling debris. He said: "Our spirit is tougher."
Because of William's decision—that moment he chose other lives over his own safety—two people are alive today.
This is what Hongkongers do. In our darkest moments, we choose others before ourselves.
A Tribute: Firefighter Ho Wai-ho
As we celebrate the survivors, we must also remember those who gave everything.
37-year-old firefighter Ho Wai-ho arrived at the scene at 3:01 PM, part of the first response team. He was attached to Sha Tin Fire Station, serving as a member of the small rescue vehicle team responsible for operating rescue equipment. At Wang Fuk Court, he was assigned to ground-floor rescue and firefighting operations.
Ho had served the Fire Services Department for about nine years. His colleagues called him "Big Strong Ho" (大隻豪), describing him as dedicated and hardworking. Before joining the Fire Services, he had been a police officer.
Around 3:30 PM, Ho lost contact with his team.
After searching for approximately 30 minutes, firefighters found him collapsed in an open area near Wang Cheong House's elevator with severe facial burns. Paramedics immediately administered first aid and rushed him to Prince of Wales Hospital.
But at 4:45 PM, Ho Wai-ho was pronounced dead.
Ho was set to marry his girlfriend of 10 years in December. His fiancée wrote on social media: "My superhero has completed his mission."
Fire Services Director Andy Yeung said: "Having been a member of the Fire Services Department for about nine years, Mr Ho's performance was valiant. I am profoundly grieved at the loss of this dedicated and gallant fireman. On behalf of all our colleagues, I have offered the deepest condolences to his family."
Hong Kong experienced her greatest sorrow, but also witnessed the purest heroism.
Ho Wai-ho, we will never forget you.
The Night of 2,000+ Rescuers
In what became Hong Kong's largest emergency response operation, over 2,000 firefighters and rescue personnel rushed into the inferno.
They didn't retreat. They didn't hesitate. They simply wanted to save lives.
These 2,000+ individuals, working non-stop for 43 hours as the fire spread across seven buildings, rescued hundreds. Remarkably, 29 hours into the fire, they still found and rescued one more survivor from a 16th-floor stairwell—a testament to their relentless determination.
The Power of Community: Present from Day One
And that wasn't all. As firefighters battled the inferno, ordinary Hongkongers sprang into action.
In an open plaza next to Wang Fuk Court, a bustling relief center emerged within three days. Hundreds of volunteers—most mobilized through Telegram and WhatsApp—began organizing donated supplies.
Volunteers from all professions and age groups worked in harmony: some sorted materials, others tracked inventory on Google Sheets, still others distributed items. A core volunteer in her twenties named Jay coordinated the entire volunteer network. She explained: "Some volunteers took on the role as the 'person-in-charge' for each type of donated item. They keep track of what we have in Google Sheets and report on what is still needed."
In this dark moment, we witnessed what "Hong Kong unity" truly means.

The Path to Recovery: You Are Not Alone
Psychologists tell us disaster recovery isn't linear. There will be good days and difficult days. This is all normal. You are not the first to experience these feelings.
For those still grieving:
Your tears are necessary. Allow yourself to feel loss, anger, fear. But remember: professional psychologists, passionate volunteers, and an entire community stand with you.
Hong Kong's major charities have established 24-hour support:
•	Samaritans Prevention Association: 24-hour crisis hotline
•	Caritas: Free counseling services for victims and families
•	Hong Kong Red Cross: Psychological first aid and long-term trauma support
For those rebuilding:
Many residents have moved into temporary housing or are staying with relatives. This is an opportunity to begin again.
Save the Children Hong Kong is collaborating with Hong Kong Shue Yan University's Department of Counselling and Psychology to provide long-term support for affected students. Multiple NGOs are also assisting families in retrieving lost documents and reissuing necessary official identification.
**The government has established an independent committee, headed by a judge, to investigate the cause and rapid spread of the fire. Police have arrested 15 people, including construction company executives and those suspected of disabling fire alarms.**Truth and justice are being pursued.

In Closing
Friends from Tai Po,
You have experienced the darkest moment of your lives. You have lost homes, loved ones, your sense of safety. These losses are real, and the pain is real.
But look at what surrounds you:
•	William Li's story, showing us human greatness
•	Ho Wai-ho and 2,000+ firefighters and rescue workers who gave everything
•	Jay and hundreds of volunteers working through the night
•	Countless strangers donating supplies and funds
•	Every Hongkonger who stopped to ask, "Are you okay?"
All of this is real too.
In the ashes, we see Hong Kong at her best. In despair, we find hope. Your recovery will inspire all of us.
Let us stand together and begin again—starting today.
As William said: "Our spirit is tougher."
This is the spirit that will carry us forward.
