These words can function independently in a sentence:

Nouns (еҗҚи©һ, Meishi)вҖӢ
Words that represent people, places, things, or concepts. Nouns do not conjugate and are the foundation of vocabulary building for learners like yourself at the N5 level.

Words that describe nouns. Japanese has two main types that behave differently:

I-adjectives (гҒ„-adjectives) вҖ“ End in гҒ„ and conjugate like verbs. Examples: ж–°гҒ—гҒ„п»ҝ (atarashii, new), еӨ§гҒҚгҒ„п»ҝ (ookii, big).вҖӢ

Adverbs (еүҜи©һ, Fukushi)вҖӢ
Words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, indicating manner, place, time, degree, or frequency. They do not conjugate.

Adnominal (йҖЈдҪ“и©һ, Rentaishi)вҖӢ
Words that express appearance or quality and function as prenominal modifiers. They directly precede nouns without requiring particles. This is a less commonly emphasized category for N5 learners.
